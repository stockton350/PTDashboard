A  Vocabulary · Health Words

Look at the words. Find each word in the dialogue below.

💊 prescription a doctor's written order for medicine
🏪 pharmacy a store where you buy medicine
🤒 symptoms signs of an illness (pain, fever, etc.)
📅 appointment a scheduled time to see a doctor
📋 insurance a plan that helps pay for health care
💉 dosage how much medicine to take
👨‍⚕️ specialist a doctor who focuses on one area of medicine
🚑 emergency room hospital area for serious or urgent care
B  Dialogue · Listen and Read 🔊

Listen to the conversation. Then read it with a partner.

DR. MILLER: Good morning. What seems to be the problem today?
PATIENT: Hi, Doctor. I've had a headache for three days, and I feel very tired.
DR. MILLER: I see. Do you have any other symptoms?
PATIENT: Yes, I also have a sore throat and a slight fever.
DR. MILLER: Let me take a look. Are you taking any medication at the moment?
PATIENT: I took some aspirin, but it didn't really help.
DR. MILLER: OK. I'm going to write you a prescription. You should rest and drink plenty of water.
PATIENT: Should I go to work tomorrow?
DR. MILLER: You shouldn't go to work for at least two days. Rest is very important.
PATIENT: Should I come back if I don't feel better?
DR. MILLER: Yes, you should call the office and make another appointment if your symptoms continue.

Think about it:

  1. What are the patient's symptoms?
  2. What does the doctor recommend?
  3. How long should the patient stay home?

C  Grammar Focus · Should / Shouldn't

Using SHOULD and SHOULDN'T for Advice

Form Example Use
You should + base verb "You should rest at home." To give positive advice
You shouldn't + base verb "You shouldn't go to work." To give negative advice
Should + subject + verb? "Should I see a specialist?" To ask for advice
⚠️ Note: SHOULD does not change with he / she / it. No -s. No -ed.
✓  "You should drink plenty of water."
✗  "You shouldn't go to work for two days."
D  Practice

Complete the sentences. Use should or shouldn't.

1. You   see a doctor if you feel worse. (should)
2. She   take her medicine twice a day. (should)
3. We   smoke — it is very bad for our health. (shouldn't)
4. He   eat a lot of sugar. (shouldn't)
5. They   call the pharmacy before picking up the prescription. (should)
6. You   worry — it is probably just a cold! (shouldn't)

Dr. K. Miller, MD    NPI: 1234567890

Take ONE (1) tablet THREE (3) times daily

  1. How many tablets should the patient take each day?
  2. When should the patient take the tablets? (before or after food?)
  3. How many days should the patient take this medicine?
  4. Can the patient get more medicine without seeing the doctor again?
  5. What warning does the label give?
